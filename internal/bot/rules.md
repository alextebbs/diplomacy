# Diplomacy — Rules

## Overview
Diplomacy is a strategy game set in pre-WWI Europe. Seven Great Powers vie for control of the continent. Unlike most war games, there are **no dice** — success depends entirely on negotiation, alliance-building, and betrayal.

## Powers
Austria, England, France, Germany, Italy, Russia, Turkey

## Map & Supply Centers
The board is divided into **75 provinces** — land, sea, and coastal. **34** of these contain **supply centers** (marked in bold on the map). Control supply centers to build and sustain units.

## Units
- **Army (A)** — moves on land
- **Fleet (F)** — moves on sea and coastal provinces

Each supply center you control lets you maintain one unit. If you have more units than centers, you must disband. Fewer units than centers means you may build new ones (only in your *home* centers).

## Turns
Each game year has up to three phases:

1. **Spring Movement** — all players submit orders simultaneously
2. **Fall Movement** — same as Spring
3. **Winter Adjustment** — build or disband units to match supply center count

If any units are dislodged during a movement phase, a **Retreat** phase occurs before the next movement.

## Orders

### Movement Phase
- **Hold** — unit stays in place
- **Move** — unit attempts to move to an adjacent province
- **Support** — unit supports another unit's hold or move (adds +1 strength)
- **Convoy** — a fleet in a sea zone transports an army across water

### Retreat Phase
Dislodged units must retreat to an adjacent empty province or be disbanded.

### Adjustment Phase (Winter)
- **Build** — place a new unit in an unoccupied home supply center
- **Disband** — remove a unit from the board

## Resolution
All orders are revealed and resolved simultaneously. Key rules:
- A move succeeds if it has more support than any opposing move or hold
- If two units move to the same province with equal strength, **both bounce** (neither moves)
- A unit being attacked cannot give support (its support is "cut")
- A unit that is dislodged must retreat or be destroyed
- Support can only be cut by a unit from a *different* province than the one being supported into

## Convoys
Armies can move across sea zones using fleets:
- The army orders a move to the destination
- Each fleet in the chain orders a convoy of that army
- If any fleet in the chain is dislodged, the convoy fails

## Victory
A player wins by controlling **18 or more supply centers** (a majority of the 34). Games can also end by draw agreement.

## Tips
- **Talk to everyone.** Alliances win games, but no alliance lasts forever.
- **Watch the board.** Count supply centers — they tell you who's winning.
- **Support your moves.** Unsupported moves into defended provinces will bounce.
- **Don't forget retreats and builds.** Missing these phases means losing units.
